Capital Gains – General Rules

1.Where do you find the general CGT rules?
 a.Income Tax Assessment Act 1936 Part IIIA.
 b.Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 Parts 3-1 and 3-3.

Correct

The correct answer is b.

From the 1998–1999 income year the 1997 Act applies.
Part 3-1 covers the general rules (ss 100-1 to 121-35).
Part 3-3 covers special rules such as rollovers (ss 122-152).
Refer to [2-5020].
2.If an amount could be taxed as income according to ordinary concepts and also as statutory income as a capital gain, which provisions take precedence?
 a.Ordinary income provisions.
 b. CGT provisions.

Correct

The correct answer is a.

Anti-overlap provisions (ss 118-20 to 118-35) provide that general assessment and exemption rules take precedence (ordinary income provisions).
Refer to [2-5020].

Averaging, Indexation and Discounts

3.Averaging ceased and indexation was frozen as from 30.9.99.
 True
 False

Correct

The correct answer is False.

Indexation was frozen as from 30 September 1999; however, averaging for individuals ceased as at 21 September 1999.
Refer to [2-5040].
4.The CGT general discount on gains potentially available for assets held for 12 months is as follows:
  • Individuals and trusts, 50%
  • Complying superannuation funds, 33.33%
  • Companies, Nil
 True
 False

Correct

The correct answer is True.

If discount option is chosen then indexation of cost base will not be available for individuals/trusts/complying superannuation funds.
Refer to [2-5045] and [2-5047].

CGT Events

5.When does CGT Event A1 (disposal) occur?
 a. When the contract to dispose of the asset is entered into.
 b. When ownership of the CGT asset passes on settlement.

Correct

The correct answer is a.

CGT Event A1 occurs when the contract is entered into. If there is no contract, then the event occurs when the ownership changes.
Refer to [2-5160].

Options

6.Which CGT event applies if a company issues share options (not under an employee share scheme) that are not exercised?
 a. Event A1 (disposal of CGT asset).
 b. Event C3 (end of an option).
 c. Event D2 (granting an option).

Correct

The correct answer is b.

Event C3 applies to options that are not exercised by the expiry date, are cancelled or abandoned.
Refer to [2-5160].
(Note: 
Share options issued under an employee share scheme are assessed under Div 83A of the Income Tax Assessment Act 1997. Refer to [2-7620].
)

Restraints of Trade

7.Where a taxpayer sells a business and subsequently enters into a restraint of trade, is the restraint of trade:
 a. A separate asset subject to Event D1 (creation of right).
 b. Part of Goodwill.
 c. Either (a) or (b).

Correct

The correct answer is c.

A restrictive covenant may be a separate asset having a value in its own right. Alternatively, it may be incidental to the sale of the business and form part of the collection of intangible elements that form part of Goodwill (TR 95/3).
Refer to [2-5170], Event D1.

Forfeited Deposits

8.Where deposits are forfeited on the sale of an asset that does not go through, is there a CGT event?
 Yes
 No

Correct

The correct answer is Yes.

Event H1 (s 104-150) deems this to be a CGT event.
Refer to [2-5210].

Offsetting Capital Losses Against Capital Gains

9.Can the CGT loss on land be offset against the gain on a depreciating asset?
 Yes
 No

Correct

The correct answer is No.

As from 21 September 1999 gains and losses on plant and equipment, although still included in the term "CGT asset", are now on revenue account. CGT losses can only be offset against CGT gains.
Refer to [2-5280].
10.Can the loss on land be offset against the gain on artwork?
 Yes
 No

Correct

The correct answer is Yes.

Although artwork falls within the category of a "collectable", gains can be offset against other CGT losses. However, losses from collectables can only be offset against gains from other collectables.
Refer to [2-5310].
11.Can the loss on a private aeroplane be offset against the gain on a personal powerboat?
 Yes
 No

Correct

The correct answer is No.

Assets being held for personal enjoyment are regarded as "personal use assets". Losses arising from the sale of such assets cannot be offset against any other gain.
Refer to [2-5320].

Cost Base of an Asset

12.Remuneration for the services of an accountant or a legal adviser can be included in the cost base of an asset.
 True
 False

Correct

The correct answer is True.

Section 110-25 of the Income Tax Assessment Act 1997 (ITAA 1997) provides the general rules on what may or may not be included in the cost base of an asset. Specifically, s 110-25(3) provides that incidental costs in acquiring the asset can be included in the cost base of an asset. Section 110-35(2) specifically includes an accountant or legal adviser as an incidental cost. However, remuneration for professional advice about the operation of ITAA 1997 is not included unless it is provided by a recognised tax advisor.
Refer to [2-5440].
13.Where a capital asset is acquired after 20.8.91, interest on borrowings to acquire that asset can be included in the cost base of an asset.
 True
 False

Correct

The correct answer is True.

Section 110-25(4) of ITAA 1997 provides that where a capital asset is acquired after 20.8.91, interest on borrowings to acquire that asset can be included in the cost base of the asset, provided that it has not already been claimed as a deduction under s 8-1 of ITAA 1997.
Refer to [2-5440].
14.The value of a taxpayer's own labour can be included in the cost base of an asset.
 True
 False

Correct

The correct answer is False.

TD 60 states that where an asset is created or constructed by a taxpayer, no value can be attributed to their own labour for inclusion in the cost base of the asset.
Refer to [2-5440].

Capital Proceeds

15.The market value substitution rule states that if no capital proceeds are received on the disposal of an asset, then:
 a. No consideration is taken to have been received.
 b. The market value of the asset is taken to have been received.
 c. The cost of the asset is taken to have been received.

Correct

The correct answer is b.

The market value substitution rule states that if no capital proceeds are received on the disposal of an asset, then the market value of the asset is taken to have been received, irrespective of whether the transaction is at arm's length.
Refer to [2-5540].

Exempt CGT Assets

16.Which of the following CGT assets is not an exempt asset?
 a. A motorcycle.
 b. Collectables acquired for $500 or less.
 c. Assets used solely to produce exempt income.
 d. Shares in a listed company.

Correct

The correct answer is d.

Shares are not exempt assets under s 118-5 of ITAA 1997.
Refer to [2-5600].

Separate CGT Assets

17.A piece of land was acquired on 1.1.80 and a building was constructed on the land on 1.1.01. Are the land and buildings separate assets for CGT purposes?
 Yes
 No

Correct

The correct answer is Yes.

Section 108-55(2) of ITAA 1997 states that a building constructed on land acquired before 20.9.85 is taken to be a separate CGT asset from the land, if you entered into a contract for the construction of the building on or after 20.9.85.
Refer to [2-5340].
18.The reduced cost base of an asset has exactly the same elements as the cost base of an asset.
 True
 False

Correct

The correct answer is False.

The third element of the reduced cost base is different. For example, the third element of the reduced cost base is any amount that is assessable because of a balancing adjustment.
Refer to [2-5460].

Main Residence Exemption

19.An individual makes a capital gain on the disposal of an apartment that has been their main residence throughout the period of the individual's ownership. Will they be subject to capital gains tax?
 Yes
 No

Correct

The correct answer is No.

A dwelling includes a unit of residential accommodation under s 118-115 of ITAA 1997.
Refer to [2-5630].

Record Keeping

20.Records in relation to CGT assets acquired after 19.9.85 should be maintained in the English language for at least five years after it becomes certain that no CGT event or further CGT event can happen to that asset.
 True
 False

Correct

The correct answer is True.

Section 121-20(2) states that records must be kept in English or readily accessible and convertible to English. Section 121-25(2) states that records must be retained until the end of five years after it becomes certain that no CGT event can happen to the asset.
Refer to [2-5732].
